## Key Findings

1. DES significantly outperforms FCFS only on large_heavy (varied, large tx sizes)
   - Blob count: 190 → 176 (-7.4%) with no delay
   - Uncompressed fill rate: 89.47% → 96.58% (+7.11pp)

2. Ordering delay improves packing further on large_heavy at the cost of latency
   - Best packing: DES+2000ms at 98.83% fill rate, -18 blobs vs FCFS
   - Latency cost: +2,273ms avg vs DES no delay

3. DES improvement is driven by compress_score reordering (smaller txs first),
   which allows the batcher to pack blob boundaries more efficiently

4. DES is workload-dependent: no benefit on uniform small tx workloads or
   already-well-packed mixed workloads
